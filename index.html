Specialty & roots

Rare and ultra-rare disease is where I go deepest, as a foundation founder and the parent of a child with an ultra-rare syndrome. Small cohorts and thin evidence are the hardest version of every service above, and the one I know best.

My methods come from a decade in infectious-disease epidemiology and geospatial science: leading multi-country field studies, designing the data collection, and adapting and extending established spatial methods to new settings, the work behind most of those 17 papers. If your work lives in that world too, all the better.

I also build the structures research runs on: I’ve founded a 501(c)(3), convened a scientific advisory board across five institutions, and grown a 200+ family research network across 17 countries. When an engagement needs the collaboration built as well as the data analyzed, that’s in scope.
